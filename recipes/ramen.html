Delicious Ramen

Ramen is a comforting and flavorful dish that brings together a rich, savory broth with perfectly cooked noodles, creating a deeply satisfying meal. Whether it’s a creamy tonkotsu, a soy-based shoyu, or a miso-infused broth, each spoonful delivers warmth and depth. The chewy noodles soak up the essence of the broth, while toppings like tender slices of pork, soft-boiled eggs, green onions, nori, and bamboo shoots add texture and bursts of flavor. With its balance of umami, spice, and heartiness, ramen is more than just a bowl of soup-it’s a soul-warming experience.

Ingredients

  • Chicken or pork bones (for tonkotsu or shoyu)
  • Miso paste (for miso ramen)
  • Soy sauce (for shoyu ramen)
  • Dashi stock or kombu (seaweed)
  • Garlic and ginger
  • Onion or scallions
  • Salt and mirin or sake (optional for depth)
  • Ramen noodles (fresh or dried, preferably alkaline noodles for authentic texture)
  • Chashu (braised pork belly or shoulder)
  • Soft-boiled eggs (ajitsuke tamago or ramen eggs)
  • Green onions (scallions)
  • Nori (seaweed sheets)
  • Bamboo shoots (menma)
  • Corn or bean sprouts (optional)
  • Mushrooms (shiitake or enoki, optional)
  • Sesame seeds or chili oil (for flavor)

Steps

  1. Prepare the broth

    • In a large pot, sauté minced garlic, ginger, and chopped onion in a bit of oil until fragrant.
    • Add chicken or pork broth (or water + bouillon), and bring to a simmer.
    • Add soy sauce, miso paste, or salt depending on your flavor base. Simmer for at least 20030 minutes for good depth. (Longer = better flavor.)

  2. Boil the eggs (optionalp)

    • Soft-boil eggs by placing them in boiling water for 6-7 minutes. Cool in ice water and peel.
    • Marinate in a mix of soy sauce, mirin, and water for extra flavor (optional).

  3. Cook the noodles

    • In a separate pot, boil ramen noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.

  4. Prepare toppings

    • Slice green onions, cook or reheat chashu pork, prepare nori, corn, mushrooms, or any other toppings you like.

  5. Assemble the ramen

    • In a bowl, place the cooked noodles. Pour over the hot broth.
    • Add toppings: sliced pork, egg halves, green onions, nori, and more.
    • Optional: drizzle chili oil, sesame seeds, or a splash of soy sauce on top.

  6. Serve and enjoy

    • Serve piping hot and enjoy your homemade ramen!
